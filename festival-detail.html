All Festival Details

Independence Day (14th August)

Pakistan celebrates its independence with parades, flag hoisting, and fireworks to honor freedom and unity.

Defence Day (6th September)

A day to commemorate the bravery of Pakistan’s armed forces who defended the nation in 1965.

Qingming (4th April)

Also known as Tomb-Sweeping Day, it is when families in China honor ancestors by cleaning graves and offering food.

Victory Day (16th December)

Commemorates Bangladesh’s victory in the 1971 Liberation War, marking the birth of the nation.

Eid-ul-Fitr

A joyful Islamic festival marking the end of Ramadan with prayers, charity, and family feasts.

Eid-ul-Adha

Also called the “Festival of Sacrifice,” it honors Prophet Ibrahim’s devotion to God through communal prayers and feasts.

Raksha Bandhan (August)

A Hindu festival celebrating the bond between brothers and sisters, symbolized by tying a rakhi on the wrist.

Halloween (31st October)

A fun-filled night of costumes, trick-or-treating, and spooky celebrations rooted in ancient Celtic traditions.

Sibi Autumn Festival

A vibrant Balochistan festival showcasing local crafts, horse shows, and folk performances celebrating heritage.

Uchal (August)

A Kalash harvest festival in Chitral Valley with dancing, singing, and offerings to thank the gods for the year’s yield.

Quetta Winter Festivals (Dec–Feb)

Celebrate Balochistan’s winter with snow sports, local music, crafts, and rich cultural performances.
